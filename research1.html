Honor Research Program: Accurate Animal Behavior Recognition Based on Traceable Video Segmentation

About OSU Honors Distinguished Research

Introduction

The theme of this project is to capture animal activity information from drone video datasets and train models that can accurately predict mammalian behavior through advanced video tracking and segmentation techniques. This project relies on knowledge and skills in deep learning and computer vision, while the state-of-the-art video segmentation technique SAM2 is used to annotate the fine segmentation of the animals in the video.

Dataset Selection

The KABR dataset includes over 10 hours of video focusing on different behavioral types for giraffes, plains zebras, and Grevy’s zebras. It consists of eight main behaviors such as walking, grazing, running, and grooming, as well as occlusion states. Additionally, the Animal Kingdom dataset provides 50 hours of annotated video for recognizing animal behavior across 850 species.

Collaborative Action Recognition Model

The CARe model performs action recognition on both visible and invisible animal species. It uses general and specific features to recognize animal behaviors by learning similarities between visible animals and predicting behaviors of unseen animals using spatially-aware weighting systems.
